# 33 PICKLEBALL

33.1 Overview

The Association of Pickleball Professionals (www.apptour.org) reported in January
2023 that 36.5 million people in the U.S. have played pickleball at least once.

There were approximately 10,300 public pickleball courts across the U.S. as of
January 2023, according to USA Pickleball (www.usapickleball.org), the national
governing body for the sport of pickleball in the U.S.

33.2 Participation

According to the _Sports, Fitness, and Leisure Activities Topline Participation_
_Report 2023_, published by the Sports & Fitness Industry Association (SFIA,
www.sfia.org), 4.8 million people played pickleball at least once in 2021 – 3.5 million
were casual participants (<8 times) and 1.4 million were core participants (8 or more
times). Participation has been as follows:

**Casual             Core             Total**

- 2016: 1.7 million 1.1 million 2.8 million

- 2017: 1.9 million 1.2 million 3.1 million

- 2018: 2.0 million 1.2 million 3.3 million

- 2019: 2.2 million 1.3 million 3.4 million

- 2020: 2.8 million 1.4 million 4.2 million

- 2021: 3.5 million 1.5 million 4.8 million

- 2022: 6.6 million 2.3 million 8.9 million

Sixty percent (60%) of regular pickleball players are age 55 and older, according
to AARP (www.aarp.org).

With 22.6% of all players, the South Atlantic Region (Delaware, Florida, Georgia,
Maryland, North Carolina, South Carolina, Virginia) has the highest number of pickleball
players, according to the SFIA. The West North Central Region (Iowa, Kansas,
Minnesota, Missouri, Nebraska, North Dakota, and South Dakota) has the fastest
growth with a 29.8% year-over-year increase in number of players in 2022.

A 2022 survey by CivicScience (www.civicscience.com) found that 12% of U.S.
adults have played pickleball and liked it; 21% had not yet played but plan to.

- Association of Pickleball Professionals (2019; www.apptour.org)

- Major League Pickleball (2021; www.majorleaguepickleball.net)

- Professional Pickleball Association (2019; www.ppatour.com)
